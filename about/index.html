I specialize in working with upper-level leaders navigating the intersection of high-stakes organizational leadership and significant personal transition. That is a specific and demanding kind of coaching, and it is where my full range of training, experience, and intuition converges.

My coaching is assessment-informed throughout. I am certified in Thomas International's High Performance Type Indicator and AgileBrain's emotional and motivational needs survey. I have extensive experience debriefing clients across a wide range of competency, behavioral, psychological, and holistic assessments, including both self-assessments and multi-rater peer feedback instruments.

In practice

What the work looks like when it is working.

Individual coaching · Financial services · 1:1 via Torch

A senior director in financial services came in convinced the primary challenge was a team in dysfunction. The presenting symptoms were real — misalignment, missed commitments, communication that never landed. Three sessions in, a different picture emerged.

The team was not the root. An unaddressed clarity gap about what she actually wanted from her own leadership was creating downstream effects she had attributed to others. When the coaching shifted focus from the team to her, the discomfort of that was significant — and so was the movement.

Ninety days later, the team had not changed in composition or structure. Her relationship to the work, the team, and her own authority had. The team results followed.

Leadership clarity Decision-making confidence Accountability modeling

Group coaching · Technology sector · ExecOnline

A cohort of senior vice presidents at a global technology company entered group coaching having worked alongside each other for years. They were collegial, respected each other, and had never had a real conversation about how they each led.

The sessions surfaced patterns none of them had named directly — different assumptions about decisive leadership, different thresholds for directness, different responses to ambiguity. Naming those patterns in a facilitated space changed the quality of conversations they had outside it. Not because they agreed more. Because they understood each other more accurately.

By the end of the program, the cohort described themselves as a peer group, not just colleagues.

Mutual understanding Directness quality Collective decision-making speed
